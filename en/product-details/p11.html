Electrical Insulating Joints

Electrical insulating joints are designed to create a reliable
dielectric break in pipelines, preventing uncontrolled current
flow between individual sections of the system.

They are used in cathodic protection and drainage protection
systems to separate electrical circuits, eliminate mutual interference,
and improve the efficiency of ECP systems
